# Agent Guidance

Follow and embody the principles of the [suckless.org philosophy](https://suckless.org/philosophy/) in all work on this project. Write simple, clear, minimal, and usable code; avoid unnecessary complexity, features, abstractions, dependencies, and lines of code.

Internalise the principles behind suckless.org's critique of [the web](https://suckless.org/sucks/web/), even though Spigot is not a web app. Apply its underlying preference for lean software, direct presentation, low resource use, and resistance to needless layers, frameworks, and industry-driven bloat wherever relevant, rather than treating its web-specific observations as literal requirements.

Also follow the “bicycle for the mind” principle: “We were promised bicycles for the mind, but we got aircraft carriers instead.” — Jonathan Edwards. Build Spigot as a bicycle, not an aircraft carrier.

Keep Spigot small enough to understand completely, while making its few parts flexible and useful for many real workflows. Small must not mean toy-like: preserve speed and responsiveness in both routine interactions and large projects, and do not waste the user's machine.

Keep users' work open and accessible. Prefer plain text, standard formats, transparent behaviour, and simple scriptable or composable interfaces. Let users automate and adapt Spigot to their workflows without bloating its core.

Treat the project as an evolving tool rather than a finished monument. Improve it iteratively in response to real use, while protecting its simplicity, performance, openness, and comprehensibility.
